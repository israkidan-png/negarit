Ethiopian law exists. Finding it is the problem.
Laws are scattered across PDFs, unofficial blogs, and expensive print volumes — with no central system tracking amendments or surfacing court precedents. The result: legal uncertainty for professionals and zero access for citizens.

The platform

What ነጋሪት does

Six tools that change how Ethiopia accesses law

📚

Centralized legal library

All federal laws, regulations, directives, and regional statutes in one searchable place — updated in real time.

🔄

Amendment tracking

See every version of every law with before/after comparisons, effective dates, and repeal history.

⚖️

Court decisions

Federal Supreme Court and High Court decisions with extracted legal principles and plain-language summaries.

🌐

Amharic & English

Full content in both languages. Key legal terms translated, explained, and cross-referenced.

📄

Legal form builder

Downloadable, editable templates for contracts, affidavits, court pleadings, and corporate documents.

📶

Offline access

Access laws without internet — built for Ethiopia's connectivity reality, including remote regions.

Who is behind this

Built by a legal professional, for Ethiopia

ኢ

Israel Woldekidan Haileyesus

Legal Professional · Legal Researcher

Israel Woldekidan is a legal professional based in Addis Ababa with over a decade of experience in legal practice and university teaching. He holds an LLM in Business and Corporate Law from Bahir Dar University and currently serves as Corporate Lawyer. He has many published peer-reviewed legal research papers and international certifications in FinTech Law, AI & Law, Privacy Law, and Digital Governance.

Israel is also the founder of ሕጉ ምን ይላል? (What Does the Law Say?) — an Amharic-language legal education channel on TikTok and YouTube, making Ethiopian law accessible to ordinary citizens. ነጋሪት is the natural extension of that mission.
